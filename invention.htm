SOPHIA

  • Sophia is a social humanoid robot developed by Hong Kong-based company Hanson Robotics

  • Sophia was activated on February 14, 2016,[2] and made her first public appearance at South by Southwest Festival (SXSW) in mid-March 2016 in Austin, Texas, United States.

  • Sophia has been covered by media around the globe and has participated in many high-profile interviews.

  • In October 2017, Sophia "became" a Saudi Arabian citizen, the first robot to receive citizenship of any country.

  • In November 2017, Sophia was named the United Nations Development Programme's first ever Innovation Champion and is the first non-human to be given any United Nation title.

  • As of 2018, Sophia's architecture includes scripting software, a chat system, and OpenCog, an AI system designed for general reasoning.

  • Sophia imitates human gestures and facial expressions and is able to answer certain questions and to make simple conversations on predefined topics (e.g. on the weather).

  • Sophia uses speech recognition technology from Alphabet Inc. (parent company of Google) and is "designed to get smarter over time".
